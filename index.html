Kian Erfaan (b. 1999) is an American law student at Fordham University School of Law, specializing in International, Comparative, and Foreign Law.

Kian’s work focuses on international legal policy, nuclear non-proliferation, and legal technology.

In 2024, Kian interned with the Lawyers Committee on Nuclear Policy at the United Nations, advocating for nuclear disarmament and global security. During his time at Fordham, he served as an environmental law editor and contributed to complex legal cases through his work at Marc J. Bern & Partners LLP.

Previously, Kian served as Vice President of Finance at Gold Medal Karate Inc., a multi-million dollar nonprofit childcare organization, where his investigations uncovered financial misconduct, leading to successful legal claims under Pennsylvania labor law. Earlier in his career, he worked as a legal assistant at Conklin Benham PC in Ann Arbor, Michigan, and conducted socioeconomic research at the University of Michigan’s Inequality Lab. Before pursuing law, he gained hands-on experience assembling computers at Mantis Computers.

Kian is currently a board member (2025–2026) of the Iranian American Bar Association’s New York Chapter and an active member of the Pennsylvania Bar Association, contributing to the Charitable Organizations, ADR, and Technology Committees. He is also affiliated with the New York State Bar Association.

His publications include Reinforcing Global Nuclear Non-Proliferation (2023) and Face-to-Face: Confronting Machine-Generated Evidence (2024), which explore intersections of law, technology, and international policy. His work has been featured by the Lawyers Committee on Nuclear Policy and Fordham School of Law.

Kian’s legal scholarship has been recognized by the UN Office of the International Association of Lawyers Against Nuclear Arms (IALANA) and academic institutions. He has presented research on sustainable materials and satellite technology at NanoDay@Penn and NASA Earth System Science forums. He frequently speaks at legal and academic conferences, including panels on ABA Opinion 512 regarding the ethical implications of AI in legal practice.

Kian earned his B.S. in Economics (with honors) from the University of Michigan in 2020 and graduated cum laude from Germantown Academy in 2017.

Outside of law, Kian is a competitive chess player with a peak USCF rating of 1424. In 2024, he actively engaged in political discourse by endorsing Robert F. Kennedy Jr. for President.
