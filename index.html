Kian E. Jamasbi is currently pursuing a Juris Doctor at Fordham School of Law with a concentration in International, Comparative, and Foreign Law. He is the co-founder and Managing Member of WorldLaw LLC.

Early Life and Education

Kian E. Jamasbi was born in Chestnut Hill. Philadelphia in 1999 and raised in Montgomery County, Pennsylvania. In 2016, he participated in the Keystone Boys State leadership program, hosted by the American Legion. As a copmetitive chess player, he represented Germantown Acdaemy at the Pennsylvania Scholastic Chess Championships and contributed to the school's undefeated season in the Lower Bucks County Scholastic Chess League. During his time at Germantown Academy, he captained the varsity men's water polo team in his junior year and competed as a shot-put specialist in track and field. In 2017, Kian graduated cum laude from Germantown Academy, earning multiple accolades. He received the NASA Earth System Science Award and the Society of Naval Engineers Award for his work on 3D-printed microsatellites and contributed to Germantown Academy's victory in the Montgomery County Science Research Competitions. For his project "Reevaluating Multinational Institutions," he was awarded the Ursinus College Mathematics Award. His creative accomplishments included receiving the Parent-Teacher Association Prize for poetry. Upon graduating, he was inducted into Mu Alpha Theta, the national mathetmatics honor society. After high school, Jamasbi began his undergraduate studies in aerospace engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park. He later transferred to the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, where he graduated with honors in 2020, earning a Bachelor of Science in Economics. He is currently completing his Juris Doctor at Fordham University School of Law, with a concentration in International, Comparative, and Foreign Law.

Career

Kian E. Jamasbi began his professional career in 2017 as an Assistant at Mantis Computers, where he supported technical operations and computer assembly. In 2018, he worked as a Sales Associate at UNIQLO USA, providing customer service during the summer. Later that year, he joined the Inequality Lab at the Institute for Social Research as a Researcher, conducting data analyses on socioeconomic disparities, a role he held until September 2019.

In 2019, Jamasbi transitioned to legal support work as a Legal Assistant at Conklin Benham PC, assisting attorneys with case reearch and document preparation. Whiel pursuing his Juris Doctor at Fordham University, he joined the Fordham Environmental Law Review as a Staff Editor in November 2020, where he reviewed and edited articles for publication until May 2021. During the same period, he worked as an Account Manager for West Publishing at Thomson Reuters, providing advanced legal research support and training to law students. In the latter half of 2021, he served as the Assistant to the Chief Operating Officer at the Connelly Foundation, supporting nonprofit operational planning and project management.

In early 2022, Kian took on a variety of roles, beginning as a Tester at Data Recognition Co., where he evaluated educational assessment systems. From March to August, worked as a Contractor for DoorDash and Instacart, maintaining high customer satisfaction in delivery services. He also served as a Buyer at Bucks County Estate Traders between April and June, evaluating and purchasing estate items for resale. In July 2022, Kian joined Marc J. Bern & Partners LLP as a Paralegal, contributing to federal litigation, including the Boys Scouts of America Chapter 11 bankruptcy case.

On September 16, 2022, Kian Jamasbi joined Gold Medal Karate Inc., operating as Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, as a Bookkeeper. In this role, he managed financial records and supported payroll operations. He was promoted to Fiscal Director in January 2023, overseeing budgeting and financial strategy, and subsequently to Vice President of Finance in July 2023. During his tenture, he uncovered significant organizational misconduct under CEO Salvatore J. Sandone, including financial fraud, labor law violations, and child endangerment. His investigations revealed the misappropriation of over $354,800 in nonprofit funds, repeated wage withholdings (including six instances impacting his own pay), and inadequate childcare sueprvision leading to a child's hospitalization. After attempting to address these issues internally, Kian submitted a 180-day resignation notice on October 12, 2023, and resigned effective October 27, 2023, following another delayed payroll.

On November 12, 2023, Kian filed for unemployment benefits, citing necessitous and compelling reasons for his resignation. On January 17, 2024, the Pennsylvania Department of Labor & Industry determined that his separation was justified under Section 402(b) of the Pennsylvania Unemployment Compensation Law. Gold Medal Karate Inc. appealed the ruling, leading to a hearing on May 15, 2024, before Labor Referee Robert Bates. In a decision issued on August 14, 2024, Referee Bates upheld Jamasbi’s eligibility for benefits, citing the employer’s erratic and delayed payments as a substantial breach of employment terms.

In 2024, Jamasbi worked as a Research Associate at the Lawyers Committee on Nuclear Policy, contributing to United Nations research on nuclear disarmament for the International Association of Lawyers Against Nuclear Arms. His work focused on advancing global security through international treaties. During this period, in October 2024, he filed a formal complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau against Goldman Sachs Bank USA for blocking over $5,100 in his Apple Savings Account. His case was escalated to the Federal Trade Commission.

WorldLaw LLC

In September 2024, Kian Jamasbi co-founded WorldLaw LLC with data scientist Marc Ivan. Headquartered in Erie, Pennsylvania, with an additional office in New York City, the company aims to modernize international arbitration through technology. Its flagship platform, WL Alpha, launched as a prototype on October 31, 2024.

Publications

Kian Jamasbi has authored several works focusing on law, technology, and global policy. His 2023 publication, Reinforcing Global Nuclear Non-Proliferation: The Case for the Fissile Material Cut-Off Treaty, was published by the Lawyers Committee on Nuclear Policy and outlines strategies for advancing nuclear disarmament. His second major work, Face-to-Face: Confronting Machine-Generated Evidence, published in 2024, examines the integration of artificial intelligence into legal proceedings and its implications for due process. In addition to academic works, Jamasbi writes extensively on arbitration, legal ethics, and technology. His articles are featured on his website and shared on X (formerly Twitter) under the handle @kianerfaan. Notable writings include Bitcoin as a Treasury Reserve Asset is a Fiduciary Duty and Pennsylvania: A Rising Hub for International Commercial Arbitration. In September 2024, he co-hosted a three-hour commentary on ABA Opinion 512, discussing ethical considerations surrounding artificial intelligence in the legal profession.

Professional Affiliations

Kian joined the Pennsylvania Bar Association in December 2023. In May 2024, he was appointed by President Nancy Conrad to serve on the Committees for Charitable Organizations and Technology. In August 2024, he was similarly appointed to serve on the Pennsylvania Bar Association's Committee on Alternative Dispute Resolution. In October 2024, Kian submitted his candidacy for the Board of Directors of the Iranian-American Bar Association's New York Chapter.

Personal Life

Kian E. Jamasbi was born in Chestnut Hill, Philadelphia, and raised in Montgomery County, Pennsylvania. Diagnosed with an arachnoid cyst in September 2024, he continues to manage his condition while maintaining an active academic and professional schedule. In July 2024, he endorsed Robert F. Kennedy Jr. for President, making a significant financial contribution to the campaign. A lifelong chess enthusiast, Kian competed in scholastic championships and remains passionate about the intellectual challenges of the game.
