Kian Erfaan (b. 1999) is an American law student, author, and entrepreneur. He is the co-founder and Managing Member of WorldLaw LLC.

Kian’s work focuses on international legal policy, nuclear non-proliferation, and legal technology. Before founding WorldLaw, he was a Research Associate at the United Nations Office of the International Association of Lawyers Against Nuclear Arms (IALANA).

From 2022 to 2023, Kian served as Vice President of Finance at Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, overseeing financial operations for a multi-million dollar nonprofit childcare organization. His investigations uncovered financial misconduct, leading to successful legal claims under Pennsylvania labor law.

Kian is a member of the Pennsylvania Bar Association, contributing to the Charitable Organizations, ADR, and Technology Committees. He is also affiliated with the Iranian American Bar Association and the New York State Bar Association.

His publications include "Reinforcing Global Nuclear Non-Proliferation" (2023) and "Face-to-Face: Confronting Machine-Generated Evidence" (2024). His writing explores law, technology, and international policy, with work featured by the Lawyers Committee on Nuclear Policy and Fordham School of Law.

Kian’s legal scholarship has been recognized by the UN Office of the IALANA and academic institutions. He has presented at NanoDay@Penn and NASA Earth System Science forums.

He frequently speaks at legal and academic conferences, including panels on ABA Opinion 512 regarding AI and ethics in legal practice.

Kian earned his B.S. in Economics (with honors) from the University of Michigan and is pursuing his Juris Doctor at Fordham University School of Law, concentrating in International, Comparative, and Foreign Law.
