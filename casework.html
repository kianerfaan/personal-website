Casework

  • In re Salvatore J. Sandone, Brave Scholar Inc., and Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts (Pa. Attorney General, Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section Oct. 16, 2024), addresses financial mismanagement, self-dealing, and labor violations under CEO Salvatore J. Sandone. The complaint incorporates findings from In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024-00130 (Pa. Dep’t of Labor & Indus., Bureau of Labor Law Compliance, Jan. 8, 2024), involving an unresolved wage claim under the Wage Payment and Collection Law, and In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. BCP-24-05-003960 (Pa. Bureau of Consumer Protection, Office of the Attorney General, Feb. 6, 2024), concerning unauthorized use of a former employee’s identity. It further references FTC Report No. 178865258 (Oct. 11, 2024), documenting $354,800 in improper financial transfers, including $142,800 funneled to Brave Scholar Inc. through a sham licensing agreement, all while Gold Medal Karate Inc. experienced wage delays, leaving employees unpaid.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, FTC Report No. 178865987 (Oct. 11, 2024), addressed labor violations and the misappropriation of $354,800 under CEO Salvatore J. Sandone, including $142,800 funneled to Brave Scholar Inc. through a sham licensing agreement. From May 2022 to September 2023, these transfers provided no benefit to the nonprofit, which faced wage delays and left employees unpaid.
  • In re Brave Scholar Inc., FTC Report No. 178865258 (Oct. 11, 2024), addressed a scheme by Salvatore J. Sandone, President of Brave Scholar Inc., misappropriating over $142,800 in nonprofit funds via a sham licensing agreement. From May 2022 to September 2023, these transfers provided no benefit to Gold Medal Karate Inc., which faced wage delays, leaving employees unpaid for multiple periods.
  • In re PA Dems, FTC Report No. 178567437 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving an unsolicited political text message encouraging immediate action on a mail-in ballot, raising concerns under the FTC’s unsolicited communication and political pressure practices jurisdiction.
  • In re PA Turnpike, FTC Report No. 178640885 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving a phishing scam that falsely claimed an outstanding toll balance, with a fabricated threat of late fees, raising concerns under the FTC's deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Yelp Services USA, FTC Report No. 178643104 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message, raising concerns under the FTC's deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Henry Jones, FTC Report No. 177789856 (Sept. 17, 2024), addressing a sextortion scam email that demanded $1,300 in cryptocurrency, representing a violation of consumer protection laws.
  • In re Adecco Recruitment, FTC Report No. 177174559 (Sept. 1, 2024), involving a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message, raising concerns under the FTC’s deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. BCP-24-05-003960 (Pa. Attorney General, Bureau of Consumer Protection, Feb. 6, 2024), involved the unauthorized use of the former Vice President of Finance’s work email and identity following their departure from the company. On June 18, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General’s Bureau of Consumer Protection stated that mediation efforts had reached an impasse. While the case remained open for potential future action, active mediation was closed, with the recommendation to pursue private legal action against Gold Medal Karate Inc. by filing in Magisterial District Court. On October 16, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General's Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section assumed control of the matter as part of a larger investigation into the activities of Salvatore Sandone.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024-00130 (Pa. Dep't of Labor & Indus., Bureau of Labor Law Compliance, Jan. 8, 2024), concerned a $3,200 wage claim, violating Pennsylvania’s Wage Payment and Collection Law. The Bureau of Labor Law Compliance closed its investigation on January 30, 2024, without addressing the claim’s merits. On October 16, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General's Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section assumed control of the matter as part of a larger probe into the activities of Salvatore Sandone.
  • Jamasbi v. Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024018904-RO (Pa. UCBR, Aug. 14, 2024), establishes that employees are protected from erratic wage payments. The case centered on the Vice President of Finance, who resigned following repeated delays in pay despite raising concerns with his employer. These delays affected multiple pay periods in July, August, and October 2023, with CEO Salvatore Sandone attributing the disruptions to issues with funders. On November 12, 2023, Jamasbi filed a claim for qualifying separation with the Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry. On January 17, 2024, the Erie UC Service Center concluded that the circumstances justified the resignation and awarded unemployment benefits. Zhang-Sah Martial Arts appealed the decision on February 5, 2024, leading to a hearing on May 15, 2024, at the Philadelphia Referee Office, attended by Sandone and Domonique Pagan. On August 14, 2024, Referee Robert Bates affirmed the UC Service Center’s decision, awarding Jamasbi $15,730.00 in unemployment compensation benefits. The deadline to appeal this ruling was September 4, 2024. Since no appeal was filed, Referee Bates' decision remains final.
