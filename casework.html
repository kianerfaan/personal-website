Casework

  • In re Goldman Sachs Bank USA, CFPB Complaint No. 241025-16669227 (Oct. 25, 2024), concerns Goldman Sachs’ blocking of over $5,100 in an Apple Savings Account, including the denial of multiple withdrawal requests, such as a nominal $1.00 attempt. The complainant, after multiple failed access attempts, incurred financial penalties, including interest charges, late fees, and credit score damage. Goldman Sachs cited unspecified "security concerns" but did not clarify or substantiate these claims. Following the complainant's feedback rejecting Goldman’s response, the CFPB closed the complaint, adding it to the Consumer Complaint Database and notifying the FTC.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, FTC Report No. 178865987 (Oct. 11, 2024), concerned labor violations and the misappropriation of at least $354,800 under CEO Salvatore J. Sandone. The report noted that $142,800 was funneled to Brave Scholar Inc., and $212,000 in childcare funds was diverted to acquire real estate at 325 Bainbridge Street.
  • In re Brave Scholar Inc., FTC Report No. 178865258 (Oct. 11, 2024), exposed a scheme by CEO Salvatore J. Sandone to misappropriate over $142,800 in nonprofit funds through a sham licensing agreement. Transfers from May 2022 to September 2023 yielded no benefit to Gold Medal Karate Inc., which faced wage delays, leaving employees unpaid for multiple periods.
  • In re PA Dems, FTC Report No. 178567437 (Oct. 7, 2024), involved unsolicited political text messages urging immediate action on a mail-in ballot.
  • In re PA Turnpike, FTC Report No. 178640885 (Oct. 7, 2024), involved a phishing scam falsely claiming an outstanding toll balance with a fabricated threat of late fees.
  • In re Yelp Services USA, FTC Report No. 178643104 (Oct. 7, 2024), concerned a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message.
  • In re Henry Jones, FTC Report No. 177789856 (Sept. 17, 2024), involved a sextortion scam email demanding $1,300 in cryptocurrency.
  • In re Adecco Recruitment, FTC Report No. 177174559 (Sept. 1, 2024), addressed a fraudulent job offer sent through unsolicited text messages.
  • Jamasbi v. Gold Medal Karate Inc. d/b/a Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024018904-RO (Pa. UC, Aug 14, 2024), holds that employees are entitled to unemployment benefits when wage payments are repeatedly delayed without resolution. The Vice President of Finance resigned after experiencing multiple pay disruptions in July, August, and October 2023, despite raising concerns with CEO Salvatore Sandone, who attributed the delays to funding issues. Jamasbi filed for unemployment on November 12, 2023, and the Erie UC Service Center ruled in his favor on January 17, 2024, finding the resignation justified. Zhang-Sah Martial Arts appealed, and a hearing was held on May 15, 2024, where both Sandone and Vice President of Operations Domonique Pagan testified. Referee Robert Bates affirmed the decision on August 14, 2024, awarding Jamasbi $15,730 in benefits. The appeal deadline expired on September 4, 2024, leaving the decision final and unchallenged.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. BCP-24-05-003960 (Pa. Attorney General, Bureau of Consumer Protection, Feb. 6, 2024), involved the unauthorized use of the former Vice President of Finance’s work email and identity. On June 18, 2024, the Bureau of Consumer Protection stated mediation efforts had failed. On October 16, 2024, the Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section took over the matter as part of a broader investigation into Salvatore J. Sandone’s activities.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. d/b/a Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024-00130 (Pa. Dep't of Labor & Indus., Bureau of Labor Law Compliance, Jan. 8, 2024), concerns a $3,200 wage claim under Pennsylvania’s WPCL. The Bureau initially concluded its investigation without addressing the claim’s merits. However, on October 8, 2024, Supervisor Paul Walters reopened the case after reviewing the August 14, 2024, decision by UC Referee Robert Bates, advising a breach of contract action through the Magisterial District Court.
