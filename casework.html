Casework

  • In re Salvatore J. Sandone, Brave Scholar Inc., and Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts (Pa. Attorney General, Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section Oct. 16, 2024), addresses financial mismanagement, self-dealing, and labor violations under Salvatore J. Sandone.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, FTC Report No. 178865987 (Oct. 11, 2024), addressed labor violations and the misappropriation of $354,800 under CEO Salvatore J. Sandone, including $142,800 funneled to Brave Scholar Inc. through a sham licensing agreement. From May 2022 to September 2023, these transfers provided no benefit to the nonprofit, which faced wage delays and left employees unpaid.
  • In re Brave Scholar Inc., FTC Report No. 178865258 (Oct. 11, 2024), addressed a scheme by Salvatore J. Sandone, President of Brave Scholar Inc., misappropriating over $142,800 in nonprofit funds via a sham licensing agreement. From May 2022 to September 2023, these transfers provided no benefit to Gold Medal Karate Inc., which faced wage delays, leaving employees unpaid for multiple periods.
  • In re PA Dems, FTC Report No. 178567437 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving an unsolicited political text message encouraging immediate action on a mail-in ballot, raising concerns under the FTC’s unsolicited communication and political pressure practices jurisdiction.
  • In re PA Turnpike, FTC Report No. 178640885 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving a phishing scam that falsely claimed an outstanding toll balance, with a fabricated threat of late fees, raising concerns under the FTC's deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Yelp Services USA, FTC Report No. 178643104 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message, raising concerns under the FTC's deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Henry Jones, FTC Report No. 177789856 (Sept. 17, 2024), addressing a sextortion scam email that demanded $1,300 in cryptocurrency, representing a violation of consumer protection laws.
  • In re Adecco Recruitment, FTC Report No. 177174559 (Sept. 1, 2024), involving a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message, raising concerns under the FTC’s deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. BCP-24-05-003960 (Pa. Attorney General, Bureau of Consumer Protection, Feb. 6, 2024), involved the unauthorized use of the former Vice President of Finance’s work email and identity following their departure from the company. On June 18, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General’s Bureau of Consumer Protection stated that mediation efforts had reached an impasse. On October 16, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General's Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section assumed control of the matter as part of a larger investigation into the activities of Salvatore Sandone.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024-00130 (Pa. Dep't of Labor & Indus., Bureau of Labor Law Compliance, Jan. 8, 2024), concerned a $3,200 wage claim, violating Pennsylvania’s Wage Payment and Collection Law. The Bureau of Labor Law Compliance closed its investigation on January 30, 2024, without addressing the claim’s merits. On October 8, 2024, the Harrisburg District Office Supervisor became seized of the matter.
  • Jamasbi v. Gold Medal Karate Inc. d/b/a Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024018904-RO (Pa. UCBR, Aug. 14, 2024)), holds that employees are entitled to unemployment benefits when wage payments are repeatedly delayed without resolution. The Vice President of Finance resigned after experiencing multiple pay disruptions in July, August, and October 2023, despite raising concerns with CEO Salvatore Sandone, who attributed the delays to funding issues. Jamasbi filed for unemployment on November 12, 2023, and the Erie UC Service Center ruled in his favor on January 17, 2024, finding the resignation justified by the circumstances. Zhang-Sah Martial Arts appealed, but after a May 15, 2024, hearing attended by Sandone and Domonique Pagan, Referee Robert Bates affirmed the decision on August 14, 2024, awarding $15,730.00 in benefits. The deadline to appeal lapsed on September 4, 2024, leaving the referee’s decision final and unchallenged.
