Casework

  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, Charitable Trusts and Organizations Complaint (Pa. Attorney General, Oct. 11, 2024), addressing the misuse of nonprofit funds and self-dealing by CEO Salvatore J. Sandone. The PA Attorney General is reviewing these violations under nonprofit and fiduciary law.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, IRS Form 13909 Complaint (Oct. 11, 2024), addressing the misuse of nonprofit funds and self-dealing by Salvatore J. Sandone, CEO. The complaint details improper financial transfers totaling $354,800.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, FTC Report No. 178865987 (Oct. 11, 2024), involving allegations of labor law violations, financial mismanagement, and misuse of nonprofit funds under the direction of Salvatore J. Sandone, CEO. The case addresses repeated payroll delays and the misuse of public funds under a $5.28 million grant from the Philadelphia School District, intended for educational programs. Additionally, $212,000 of these funds was diverted to finance a real estate acquisition.
  • In re Brave Scholar Inc., FTC Report No. 178865258 (Oct. 11, 2024), addressing a fraudulent scheme by Salvatore J. Sandone, President of Brave Scholar Inc., involving the misappropriation of nonprofit public funds under the guise of a fraudulent licensing agreement. Between May 2022 and September 2023, over $142,800 was transferred from Gold Medal Karate Inc. to Brave Scholar Inc. for supposed "curriculum technology" licenses, despite no discernible benefit to the nonprofit. These transfers occurred while Gold Medal Karate Inc. was simultaneously experiencing wage delays, leaving employees unpaid for multiple pay periods.
  • In re PA Dems, FTC Report No. 178567437 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving an unsolicited political text message encouraging immediate action on a mail-in ballot, raising concerns under the FTC’s unsolicited communication and political pressure practices jurisdiction.
  • In re PA Turnpike, FTC Report No. 178640885 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving a phishing scam that falsely claimed an outstanding toll balance, with a fabricated threat of late fees, raising concerns under the FTC's deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Yelp Services USA, FTC Report No. 178643104 (Oct. 7, 2024), involving a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message, raising concerns under the FTC's deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Henry Jones, FTC Report No. 177789856 (Sept. 17, 2024), addressing a sextortion scam email that demanded $1,300 in cryptocurrency, representing a violation of consumer protection laws.
  • In re Adecco Recruitment, FTC Report No. 177174559 (Sept. 1, 2024), involving a suspected fraudulent job offer delivered via unsolicited text message, raising concerns under the FTC’s deceptive practices jurisdiction.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. BCP-24-05-003960 (Pa. Bureau of Consumer Protection, Office of the Attorney General, Feb. 6, 2024), involving the unauthorized use of a former employee's work email and identity following their departure from the company, in violation of consumer protection statutes. On June 18, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General’s Bureau of Consumer Protection sent a formal letter stating that the mediation process had reached an impasse. While the case file will remain open for potential future action, the Bureau has closed active mediation efforts. The ongoing recommendation is to pursue private legal action against Gold Medal Karate Inc. by filing a lawsuit in Magisterial District Court.
  • In re Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024-00130 (Pa. Dep't of Labor & Indus., Bureau of Labor Law Compliance, Jan. 8, 2024), concerned a $3,200 wage claim alleging the company’s failure to pay legally owed wages, potentially violating Pennsylvania’s Wage Payment and Collection Law.
    The Bureau of Labor Law Compliance closed its investigation on January 30, 2024, without addressing the claim’s merits. However, on October 16, 2024, the Pennsylvania Attorney General's Charitable Trusts and Organizations Section assumed control of the matter as part of a larger probe into the activities of Salvatore Sandone.
  • Jamasbi v. Gold Medal Karate Inc. dba Zhang-Sah Martial Arts, No. 2024018904-RO (Pa. UCBR, Nov. 12, 2023), establishes that employees are protected from erratic wage payments. The case centered on the Vice President of Finance, who resigned following repeated delays in pay despite raising concerns with his employer. These delays affected multiple pay periods in July, August, and October 2023, with CEO Salvatore Sandone attributing the disruptions to issues with funders.
    On November 12, 2023, the employee filed a claim for qualifying separation with the Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry. On January 17, 2024, the Erie UC Service Center concluded that the circumstances justified the resignation and awarded unemployment benefits. Zhang-Sah Martial Arts appealed the decision on February 5, 2024, leading to a hearing on May 15, 2024, at the Philadelphia Referee Office, attended by Sandone and Domonique Pagan on behalf of the employer. On August 14, 2024, Referee Robert Bates affirmed the UC Service Center’s decision, awarding Jamasbi $15,730.00 in unemployment compensation benefits, effective October 26, 2023. The deadline to appeal this ruling was September 4, 2024. Since no appeal was filed, Referee Bates' decision remains final.
